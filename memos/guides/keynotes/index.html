Submitting your deck

  • · Upload your native format with fonts (ttf/otf) and media embedded — not a PDF or SlideShare link.
  • · Upload even if incomplete — mark it WIP. The AV team stages and tests from this version.
  • · Bring a USB copy of your final deck to rehearsal as a backstop.
  • · Submit via the Google Drive link before EOD [TBD].

Audio & the house system

  • · Playing audio or video with sound? Flag it on the AV questionnaire — this is the most-missed item, and you must hold the rights to anything you play.
  • · You present from the house computer and advance with the provided slide advancer.
  • · Want to present from your own laptop instead? Tell us on the AV form so we can plan for it.
🎬

Rehearsal — Required

🎤

Every keynote speaker rehearses. No exceptions.

Rehearsal is the only time you'll run your deck on the house computer with the confidence monitor and walk-on before you're live. It's how we catch surprises — not on show day.

When

At Moscone West on [TBD date]. We rehearse in open blocks — e.g. a morning block and an afternoon block — and you drop in anytime within your assigned window. Your slot runs about 15 minutes.

What to bring

Everything you'll present with — laptop, adapters, and a USB copy of your deck. We'll have radio comms to the AV team throughout.

Your block

We'll email your rehearsal window and a calendar invite. Reply right away if it doesn't work — locking these blocks early is what keeps the day on schedule.

Show time

Stage Direction & Main Stage Guide

🗺️

Coming soon: a render of the keynote stage from the audience and the floor plan, so you know exactly what you're walking into. We'll drop it here once production finalizes the layout.

Walk-on & walk-off

  • · Be in the green room 30 min before your talk.
  • · Walk to the center-stage mark — your mic is live the moment you hit the stage.
  • · Finish, take in the applause, then exit the way you came on.

Mic & slide advancer

  • · Go to the audio tech during the break before your block to get mic'd up.
  • · Use the house slide advancer from the house computer.

Confidence monitor & timer

  • · Two downstage monitors: one mirrors slides, one shows speaker notes.
  • · Notes show only from the house computer + advancer.
  • · A timer counts down from 18 minutes.

Presenting from your laptop?

  • · Only if you've cleared it on the AV form. Bring it to the tech table during the break before your talk.
  • · Your speaker notes won't show on the downstage monitors — but will show in Presenter Mode on your own screen.

Stage presence

We strongly recommend stepping away from the podium and using the house computer + advancer:

  • · Connect with the audience
  • · Looks more professional
  • · Cleaner changeovers
  • · It's more fun!

Arrival & time limits

  • · Arrive 30 min before the block starts — not your talk time. If your talk is 10:00 but the block opens at 9:00, arrive by 8:30. Last in the block? You may need to arrive earlier.
  • · Talks are 18 minutes (schedule says 20 to allow a 2-min changeover). These are hard limits — going over risks costly union overtime, and we'll have to play you off.

On camera

Wardrobe

Keynotes are recorded and become your most-watched moment on YouTube — dress with the camera in mind.

Do

  • · Wear solid, neutral colors that complement your skin tone
  • · Keep outfits simple, without too many accessories
  • · Choose clothes that are comfortable and authentically you

Don't

  • · Dangly earrings / chunky necklaces — they hit the mic
  • · Small patterns — they look fuzzy on camera
  • · Large patterns or prominent logos — distracting
  • · All black or all white — harsh, fights the lighting
  • · Short skirts — fireside/panel seating can be uncomfortable
