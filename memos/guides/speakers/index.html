Presenting

What happens in the room

The most common question is "where do I present from?" — from your own laptop, every time.

Your laptop, plug-and-play

  • - Present from your own laptop — HDMI is the standard room connection.
  • - Bring your own adapter or dongle if your laptop needs one.
  • - Nothing to upload in advance, and no compatibility check — you present what you want.
  • - Bring a USB copy of your slides as a just-in-case backup.

Audio, demos & AV support

  • - Playing audio or video with sound? Tell the AV team in advance — and make sure you hold the rights to it.
  • - An AV tech is in each room for mics, screens, and levels.
  • - For live demos, have a local or recorded backup ready.

Timing & arrival

  • - Arrive at least 30 minutes before the block starts, not your exact talk time.
  • - Later slot in the block? You may still need to arrive earlier.
  • - Talk lengths are hard limits — running long risks union overtime and we may play you off.
ℹ️

No confidence monitor in breakout, workshop, or expo rooms.

The confidence monitor and house computer live on the keynote stage only. In your room, keep your notes in Presenter Mode on your own laptop. If you need a specific display setup, confirm it with the room's AV tech before your block.
